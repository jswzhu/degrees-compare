CurriculumThe M.A. in Public Policy uses an interdisciplinary approach to study and analyse contemporary political, economic, and social issues in a comprehensive manner. The curriculum is carefully structured to impart strong training in policy theory, covering key literature and policy debates. You will develop and build on your quantitative and qualitative analytical skills and receive exposure to the real-world policy making and related management process. The curriculum comprises foundational, essential, and elective courses. Each course accounts for four credits. These, along with two projects (a group and a solo project) which account for eight credits each, add up to the total credit requirement for the degree.Foundation/Pre-session CoursesEconomics in Public PolicySociety and Public Policy (An introduction to public policy and the idea of social transformation)Law and PolicyEssential CoursesMicroeconomicsMacroeconomicsGender and DevelopmentInternational Organisations and Policy RegimesPublic Finance and ManagementStatistical Methods and Data AnalysisQualitative Analysis and Survey DesignProgram Design and EvaluationElective CoursesStudents are required to opt for five electives which will fall under the following specializations.
